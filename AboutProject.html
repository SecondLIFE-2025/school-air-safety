Project Aim

The aim of this project is to develop a simple, clear, and practical digital system that translates air quality data into actionable school safety recommendations during periods of poor air quality.

Why This Project Is Important

In many cities of Kazakhstan, winter air pollution is a long-term issue. Despite available monitoring data, this information is rarely adapted for school-level decision-making. As a result, students continue outdoor activities even during highly polluted days.

Methodology

  • Analysis of official air quality monitoring data
  • Focus on PM2.5 as the most harmful pollutant
  • Survey of students, parents, and school staff
  • Comparison of environmental data with social response

Limitations

The project is limited by a small number of survey responses and a short monitoring period. The system currently provides general recommendations rather than city-specific automated alerts.

Future Improvements

  • Integration of real-time air quality data
  • Automatic alerts for schools and parents
  • Expansion to multiple cities and countries
  • Mobile application version of the dashboard
