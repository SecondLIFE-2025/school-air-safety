Air Quality Monitoring Results

Atmospheric air quality was monitored near Nazarbayev University in Astana during a 7-day winter period. The analysis focused on carbon monoxide (CO), particulate matter (PM10 and PM2.5), and sulfur dioxide (SO₂).

Key Environmental Findings

  • PM2.5 concentrations exceeded safe limits on all monitored days
  • Pollution levels remained consistently high, not episodic
  • Carbon monoxide showed daily fluctuations linked to human activity
  • Winter heating and traffic are dominant pollution sources

Survey Results: School & Public Perception

An online survey was conducted among students, parents, and school staff from different regions of Kazakhstan.

  • Most respondents described winter air quality as poor or very poor
  • 50% reported noticing smog or pollution 1–2 times per week
  • Coal heating and traffic were identified as main pollution sources
  • Fatigue, headaches, and breathing difficulties were common symptoms
  • 70% stated that schools do not cancel outdoor activities during pollution
  • 80% supported the idea of a daily school air safety report

Data Interpretation

The combination of monitoring data and survey responses reveals a mismatch between actual pollution levels and school responses. While pollution is persistent and severe during winter, protective measures in schools remain limited or unclear.
